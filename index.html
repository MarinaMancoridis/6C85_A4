Marina Mancoridis — marinam@mit.edu

Proposition: Between 1995 and 2019, the economic value of China’s rail system expanded at a rate that significantly surpassed that of the United States.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Rationale 1 (Data Transformation): I made the decision to plot the running sum of the average value of the railroads. This intentionally misleads the viewer into thinking that the growth in China's rail system value is exponential. The cumulative sum naturally produces an exponential-like curve, subtly misleading the viewer into perceiving a steeper increase than what truly exists. I score this decision as -1.5 because the data transformation is inherently misleading in a way that requires careful analysis to recognize.
  • Rationale 2 (Textual Content): Including factual content in text boxes enhances the perception that the visualization is well-researched. However, the facts were selectively chosen to strengthen the argument—highlighting more positive aspects and higher-frequency data about China's rail system compared to the United States. I score this decision as -0.5 because, while the information is factual and adds valuable historical context to the illustration, it is selectively presented in a way that introduces bias.
  • Rationale 3 (Icons): The image features only two icons: a high-speed train emoji on the China line and a toy train emoji on the United States line. This visual choice subtly suggests that the U.S. rail system is outdated or inferior compared to China’s, influencing the viewer’s perception of their relative economic progress. I rate this decision -0.5 because it subtly guides the reader toward a conclusion about the two rail systems, though its impact remains relatively minor.
  • Rationale 4 (Summarization): In the paragraph beneath the title, I explicitly summarize the conclusion the reader is "supposed" to draw from my visualization, using strategic language—describing China’s growth as "an almost exponential surge" and the United States' trend as "relatively stagnant." This wording not only guides the reader toward a predetermined interpretation rather than letting them form their own conclusions, but it is also factually misleading: it fails to mention that the visualization is based on a running sum, a critical detail that is only subtly noted in italics under the left axis title. I rate this decision -1.5 because it significantly misrepresents the data while warping the viewer's perception.
  • Rationale 5 (Plot Type): I chose a line chart to represent the data, a common and familiar type of visualization that is easy to interpret. This choice is meant to convince the viewer that the data is straightforward and easy to understand, even though the running sum data transformation makes the visualization inherently misleading. I rate this decision -0.5 because the line chart is a common and appropriate choice for this type of data, but it is used here to mask the underlying data transformation that makes the visualization deceptive.

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Rationale 1 (Data Transformation): The most significant decision I made was to plot the percentage difference between consecutive years in the average value of railways for China and the United States on the y-axis. This metric is intentionally difficult to interpret, and I further complicated it by orienting the y-axis sideways and displaying details in small italics; these design choices obscure the fact that the U.S. rail system consistently had negative growth, while China’s was frequently above 0%. I rate this decision -1.5 because it makes the data harder to understand and subtly hides a key comparison.
  • Rationale 2 (Chart Type): Similarly to the last visualation, I chose a line chart to represent the data because it is a common and familiar type of chart. This choice allows me to mask any deceptive design decisions under the guise of a simple and straightforward visualization. I rate this decision -0.5 because the line chart is an appropriate choice for this type of data, but it is used here to hide the fact that the visualization is inherently misleading.
  • Rationale 3: The emphasis on the drop-off point in the text and title distracts from a more critical trend: China consistently maintained positive growth, while the U.S. was often below 0%. This framing shifts the reader’s focus away from the long-term disparity in growth and toward a single dramatic event. I rate this decision -1.0 because it doesn't misrepresent the data, but it does lead to a misleading interpretation.
  • Rationale 4 (Summarization): I guide the reader's interpretation in the sentence beneath the title, effectively drawing conclusions for them rather than letting them analyze the data independently. To make matters more deceptive, throughout the text, I use misleading or vague terminology to frame the same information— for example, referring to changes as "growth momentum", or "investment in railway systems" to shape the reader's perception. I rate this decision -1.5 because it significantly misrepresents the data while warping the viewer's perception.
  • Rationale 5 (Data Omission): I deliberately excluded a key data point—China's 2019 value—from the visualization; this omission is critical because the analysis relies on the narrative that the U.S. trend plummeted a year later. If China had experienced a spike in 2019, it could have dramatically altered the perceived story. I rate this decision -2 because it significantly misrepresents the data and skews the viewer’s perception.
